About the Tripartite Struggle

This guide explores the Tripartite Struggle, a key period in medieval Indian history defined by the conflict between the Palas, Gurjara-Pratiharas, and Rashtrakutas. The primary objective was control over **Kannauj** and the fertile Gangetic Doab, which symbolized sovereignty. This mutual weakening ultimately paved the way for later invasions from the North-West. Summary of Conflict

  • Objective: Control **Kannauj** and the fertile Gangetic Doab.
  • Parties: Palas vs. Pratiharas vs. Rashtrakutas.
  • Result: Mutual weakening of all three dynasties, which paved the way for Islamic invasions from the North-West.

The Palas (East)

The Pala dynasty ruled from the East (Bengal/Bihar) and were known for their patronage of Buddhism and major universities. This section covers their key rulers, expansion, and cultural contributions. Use the sub-tabs below to explore specific facts.

  • Founder: **Gopala** (Elected by the people to end anarchy).
  • Key Rulers: Dharmapala, Devapala.
  • Decline: Succeeded by the Sena Dynasty.
  • **Dharmapala:** Initiated the Tripartite Struggle for Kannauj.
  • **Devapala:** Blocked from westward expansion by the Pratiharas; expanded **East** into Odisha, Assam (Pragjyotishpur), and parts of Nepal.
  • **Dharmapala** revived **Nalanda University**.
  • **Dharmapala** also founded **Vikramshila University**.
  • They were prominent patrons of Mahayana Buddhism.
  • Maintained strong cultural and trade ties with Tibet (due to Buddhism).
  • Strong links with **Southeast Asia**, specifically the **Shailendra Dynasty** (Java, Sumatra, Malaya).

The Gurjara-Pratiharas (North/West)

The Gurjara-Pratiharas ruled from the North & West, acting as a vital bulwark against foreign invasions for a long period. This section covers their military role, key rulers, and cultural facts. Use the sub-tabs below to explore specific facts.

  • Founder: **Nagabhatta I**.
  • Key Rulers: Vatsaraja, Nagabhatta II, Mihira Bhoja.
  • Decline: Broke up into independent Rajput states (Chandelas, Chauhans, etc.).
  • Acted as a bulwark against foreign invaders.
  • Repeatedly repulsed **Arab attacks** from the west.
  • *Note:* There is **no mention of war chariots** recorded in their army.
  • **Nagabhatta II:** Revived the dynasty after early defeats.
  • **Mihira Bhoja:** Devotee of Vishnu; famously took the title **Adi Varaha**.
  • The Sanskrit poet **Rajasekhara** was in the court of **Mahendrapala I**.

The Rashtrakutas (Deccan)

The Rashtrakutas dominated the Deccan and famously fought on two fronts: against the northern powers for Kannauj and against the southern powers like the Cholas. This section covers their key rulers, warfare, and cultural tolerance. Use the sub-tabs below to explore specific facts.

  • Founder: **Dantidurga**.
  • Key Rulers: Amoghavarsha, Krishna III.
  • Decline: Overthrown by the Chalukyas of Kalyani.
  • Continuously fought on two fronts:
    • **North:** Against Palas and Pratiharas.
    • **South:** Against Pallavas and Cholas.
  • **Krishna III** defeated the Chola ruler **Parantaka I** (at the Battle of Takkolam).
  • This victory led to the annexation of the northern Chola empire.
  • **Amoghavarsha:** A Jain scholar who wrote the **first Kannada book on poetics** (*Kavirajamarga*).
  • **Religious Tolerance:** Known for being generally tolerant, they allowed **Muslim traders** to settle and preach within their kingdom to foster trade.
